## FINAL PROMPT

---

You are a senior backend game systems architect. Build a complete, production-ready game engine in pure JavaScript for a Mendikot-style multiplayer trick-taking card game. This is logic only — no UI, no rendering, no server framework.

---

### ARCHITECTURE CONSTRAINTS

- All state is represented as a single immutable `GameState` object
- Every function is a pure state transition `(GameState, input) → { success boolean, state GameState, error string }`
- No global variables. No mutations. Spreadcopy state at every level
- No shortcuts. Handle every edge case explicitly
- All logic is deterministic
- Validation, rule evaluation, and state mutation must be in separate layers

---

### PLAYER COUNTS & DECK RULES

4 Players
- 1 standard deck A K Q J 10 9 8 7 6 5 4 3 2 per suit × 4 suits = 52 cards
- Each player gets 13 cards → 13 tricks per match
- Mindis (10s) 4 total (one per suit)
- Minimum bid 8  Maximum bid 13

6 Players
- 2 decks combined, cards 6–A only 9 ranks × 4 suits × 2 = 72 cards
- Each player gets 12 cards → 12 tricks per match
- Mindis (10s) 8 total (two per suit across two decks)
- Minimum bid 7  Maximum bid 12

8 Players
- 2 decks combined, cards 6–A only 9 ranks × 4 suits × 2 = 72 cards
- Each player gets 9 cards → 9 tricks per match
- Mindis (10s) 8 total
- Minimum bid 7  Maximum bid 9

Rank Order (high → low)
- 68 players `A  K  Q  J  10  9  8  7  6`
- 4 players `A  K  Q  J  10  9  8  7  6  5  4  3  2`

---

### TEAM ASSIGNMENT

- Players choose their own seat number when joining a room (1 through N)
- Teams are determined by alternating seat parity
  - Team A = odd seats (1, 3, 5, 7)
  - Team B = even seats (2, 4, 6, 8)
- Seating is fixed for the lifetime of the room
- If a seat is already taken, return `{ success false, error 'SEAT_TAKEN' }`
- Game cannot start until all seats are filled

---

### DEALING RULES

- First match of a room either team may deal (the first player to trigger `startDeal()` initiates it; their team is the dealer)
- All subsequent matches the losing team deals
- Dealer is tracked as a `teamId` in `GameState`
- After deal, the winning team (non-dealer team in match 1; previous match winner thereafter) selects one card to hide (the Hidden Card)

---

### HIDDEN CARD SYSTEM

- After cards are dealt, any one player from the hiding team calls `selectHiddenCard(playerId, cardIndex)` to nominate the hidden card
- The hidden card is removed from that player's visible hand and stored in `GameState.hiddenCard`
- In Contract Mode the bidding winner (and only them) may call `peekHiddenCard(playerId)` to see the hidden card's value before play starts. All others cannot see it
- In Mindi Mode the hidden card is concealed from all players until revealed
- The player who holds the hidden card (the player who selected it) leads the first trick
- Revealing the hidden card During any trick, if a player cannot follow the led suit, they may call `requestHiddenCardReveal()`. The hidden card is immediately revealed to all players. The suit of the hidden card becomes the trump suit for the remainder of the match
- The hidden card can only be revealed once per match. If already revealed, `requestHiddenCardReveal()` returns `{ success false, error 'ALREADY_REVEALED' }`
- If the hidden card is never revealed, there is no trump suit for that match

---

### BIDDING PHASE

Process
- Bidding is simultaneous and open — all players may bid at any time during the bidding window
- The engine tracks a `biddingDeadlineTimestamp` (set by the caller in ms). The engine does not run timers itself — the caller is responsible for calling `closeBidding(gameState)` after the deadline
- Any player may call `submitBid(playerId, amount)` at any time while bidding is open
- A bid must be strictly greater than the current highest bid and within the valid range for the player count
- A player may call `passBid(playerId)` to opt out; a passed player cannot bid again this round
- The highest bid at close wins. If two bids are equal (race condition), the earlier timestamp wins
- The winning bidder's team wins the contract. The winning bidder themselves chooses the trump suit via `selectTrumpSuit(playerId, suit)` before play begins

If all players pass (no bid made)
- `closeBidding()` auto-sets `gameMode = 'MINDI'`
- No trump suit is declared at this stage (trump emerges only if hidden card is revealed)

---

### GAME MODES

Contract Mode (at least one bid was made)
- The bidding winner's team must win ≥ bid number of tricks
- If they win ≥ bid tricks → bidding team wins the match
- If they win  bid tricks → opposing team wins the match

Mindi Mode (all players passed)
- No bid, no declared trump
- The team that wins the majority of mindis (10s) wins the match
- 4 players majority = 3 or more out of 4
- 68 players majority = 5 or more out of 8
- If mindis are split exactly equally (only possible in 4-player 2–2), the match is a draw — no winner; the same team deals again

---

### TRICK RULES

1. The player with the hidden card leads the first trick
2. Each subsequent trick is led by the winner of the previous trick
3. Players must follow the led suit if they have a card of that suit in hand
4. If a player cannot follow suit, they may play any card (and may trigger `requestHiddenCardReveal()` at this moment)
5. Trump beats non-trump. Among trump cards, highest rank wins
6. If no trump is played, highest card of the led suit wins
7. Tiebreaker for duplicate cards the card played first (earlier in play order) wins
8. The trick winner captures all cards in the trick. If any captured card is a 10, it counts as a mindi for that winner's team

Validate on every `playCard()` call
- It is this player's turn (`WRONG_TURN`)
- The card exists in the player's hand (`CARD_NOT_IN_HAND`)
- Suit-following rule is respected (`MUST_FOLLOW_SUIT`)
- The match is in the playing phase (`WRONG_PHASE`)

---

### MATCH RESULT & RESET

After all tricks are played, `resolveMatch()` must
1. Count tricks won per team
2. Count mindis captured per team
3. In Contract Mode compare tricks won by bidding team vs. the bid
4. In Mindi Mode compare mindi counts
5. Determine match winner (or draw)
6. Record `matchResult` in `GameState`
7. Set `nextDealer` = losing team (or same team on draw)

`resetMatch()` must
1. Clear all hands, trick history, bid state, hidden card, trump suit, play order
2. Retain room config, player seats, team assignments, cumulative match winloss record
3. Set phase back to `DEALING`

---

### GAMESTATE SCHEMA

Define a complete `GameState` object including at minimum

```
roomId, playerCount, phase, teams, players, seats,
deck, hands (per player), hiddenCard, hiddenCardRevealed,
trumpSuit, gameMode, biddingState, currentTrick,

```

`phase` must be one of
`WAITING  DEALING  HIDING_CARD  BIDDING  TRUMP_SELECTION  PLAYING  RESOLVING  COMPLETE`
